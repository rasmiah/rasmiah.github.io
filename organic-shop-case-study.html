Organic Shop — Grocery App

A fresh, green grocery shopping experience with clear categories, soft gradients, and friendly visuals from onboarding to delivery tracking.

Role: UI/UX Designer

Tools: Figma

Platform: Mobile (iOS / Android)

Explore in Figma

  • Design file: View Figma design file

Problem

Many grocery apps feel crowded and stressful, with long lists, unclear hierarchy, and too many actions on each screen. It’s hard to quickly find everyday items.

Goal

Design a calm, structured organic grocery app where users can move smoothly from splash and onboarding to browsing, product details, and delivery tracking.

Target User

Busy people who order fresh groceries from their phone and prefer a clean interface, soft visuals, and clear structure instead of a noisy shopping experience.

Authentication

Login & Register

I explored three login and three register layouts, then chose a centered version with simple fields, one primary action, and soft green accents. The focus is on fast, familiar sign-in inside a softer visual system.

Onboarding Flow

A three-screen onboarding continues one story across all pages: what the app does, why it focuses on organic products, and how fast delivery works. Linear gradients, imagery, and short copy set a friendly tone before the main shop.

Browsing & Categories

The app has a main shop view and a focused category screen. Categories can be shown as tall vertical cards or as a compact two-column grid, but both layouts reuse the same card component, spacing, and shadows for consistency.

Product Details & Cart

Product screens focus on the image, price, and quantity controls, with similar products underneath to keep the user exploring. When an item is added, a bottom notification confirms it and offers two clear choices: continue shopping or go to checkout.

Delivery Tracking

The delivery view shows the route on a map and a bottom sheet with address, driver, and ETA. I designed a simple map-only state and an expanded sheet state so users can check details without losing context of the path.

Navigation

A side navigation drawer groups the main areas of the app in one place, keeping the shopping screens focused on products. It follows the same corner radius, shadows, and green highlights as the rest of the system.

Design Decisions

Visual Style

The palette is built around fresh greens with white and a soft grey as neutrals. I used gradients, imagery, and a separate background practice page to experiment with shadows and color combinations before final screens.

Components

A dedicated components page includes primary and secondary buttons, checkboxes, toggles, inputs, a slider, and a two-button toggle where the active option becomes primary. These pieces are reused across login, categories, product cards, navigation, and bottom sheets.

Shadows & Micro-interactions

Shadows were a core focus: they lift cards, bottom sheets, and notifications without heavy borders. Combined with subtle state changes on buttons and toggles, they make the interface feel layered and responsive while staying calm and minimal.
