Flux — NFT Landing Page

A one-page NFT website for discovering, buying, and tracking collections with bold visuals and clear calls to action.

Role: UI/UX Designer

Tools: Figma

Platform: Web (Desktop)

Explore in Figma

  • Design file: View Figma design file

Problem

Many NFT sites feel noisy and overloaded, making it hard for users to understand the value of the platform quickly.

Goal

Create a single landing page that clearly explains what Flux does, shows trust and analytics, and ends with a strong call to action.

Concept

Flux brings everything into one scroll: hero, “Featured on”, analytics, app promotion, testimonials, and a final “Are you ready?” section.

Hero & Navigation

The hero pairs “Discover and collect NFTs” with two CTAs: Buy NFTs (primary) and Sell NFTs (secondary). A simple nav (Crypto, About, Pricing, Contact, Buy NFTs) hints at the content below.

Featured & Analytics

A “Featured on” strip adds credibility, followed by an analytics block with “Built-in analytics to track your NFTs” and a View our pricing button to connect insight with action.

Get Our App

The “Get our app” section invites users to browse NFTs from their phone, with a clear headline, app mockup, and one primary CTA: Download our app.

Testimonials

Three short testimonial cards add social proof and break up the layout before the final decision section.

Final Call to Action

The last card asks “Are you ready?” with a single primary button, Get Started, giving the page a clear end point.

Design Decisions

Visual Style

I used a deep indigo base with bright accents and dark blue . White and black keep text sharp against the background.

Layout & Sections

The page follows a clear story: hero, trust, analytics, app, testimonials, then one final CTA. Each block keeps the same spacing, card radius, and button style.

Buttons & Hierarchy

All main actions are primary buttons: Buy NFTs, View our pricing, Download our app, and Get Started. This makes the next step obvious at every section.
